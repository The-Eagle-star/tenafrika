#Welcome To TenAfrika

Problem Statement

In Kigali’s Nyarugenge district, climate change is intensifying deforestation and irregular rainfall, causing severe soil erosion, flooding, food insecurity, and rising urban temperatures. These problems worsen during the rainy seasons, damaging homes and infrastructure. Nyarugenge’s vulnerability is heightened by limited tree cover, poor waste management, and weak land use planning. Overreliance on wood fuel—accounting for about 83% of Rwanda’s total energy use and over 90% in rural areas (Ministry of Environment, 2023)—along with widespread tree cutting, are key drivers of environmental degradation. Weak urban planning and rapid, unregulated urbanization further increase flood risk and ecosystem loss. This threatens national sustainability goals, including Rwanda’s Vision 2050, and endangers public health and urban resilience. Urgent, coordinated action is needed in Nyarugenge to restore green spaces, promote clean energy, improve waste management, and empower communities to build a climate-resilient, healthier urban environment.

Solution Statement

We want to introduce and spread eco-friendly charcoal briquettes in Nyarugenge as a clean energy solution to reduce heavy use of wood fuel. These briquettes are made from waste like sawdust or crop leftovers, burn longer with less smoke, and help fight deforestation and health issues caused by indoor air pollution. They're cheap, easy to make with simple machines (Manual Briquette Press) and sold locally through cooperatives or community hubs. The goal is to train youth and women to produce and sell them, creating jobs, while also protecting the environment. Groups like REMA can help support this effort, and education programs will teach families how to switch from firewood. This solution is not only affordable but also helps poor families, save trees, and build a cleaner, greener future for everyone.
